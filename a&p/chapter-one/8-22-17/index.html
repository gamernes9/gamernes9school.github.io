Homeostasis

Homeostasis describes the body's ability to maintain relatively stable internal conditions even though the outside world is continuously changing.

Homeostatic Controls

The elements of a homeostatic control system.

  1. Stimulus produces change in variable.
  2. Receptor detects change.
  3. Input: information is sent along afferent pathway to control center.
  4. Output: information is senta long efferent pathway to effector.
  5. Response of effector feeds back to reduce the effect of stimulus and returns variable to homeostatic level.

Negative feedback mechanisms react negatively to a stimulus affecting our body.

  • Most homeostatic control mechanisms are negative feedback mechanisms.

Positive feedback mechanisms react positively to a stimulus affecting our body.

  • Positive feedback mechanisms are rare in the body because they tend to increase the original disturbance (stimulus) and to push the variable farther from its original value.

ADDENDUM - ADDED 8-23-17

Stimulus -> receptor -> input -> control center -> output -> effector -> response

Afferent pathway: receptor -> input

Efferent pathway: control center -> output -> effector -> response
