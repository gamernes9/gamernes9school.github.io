9-1-17 Chapter 2

Matter: anything that takes up space and has mass

Energy:has no mass and does not take up space; the ability to do work or to put matter into motion

  • Kinetic: when energy is actually doing work (moving objects)
  • Potential: when energy is inactive or stored

* Energy cannot be created nor destroyed; only changed from one form to another

Forms of energy:

  • Chemical: stored in the bonds of chemical substances
  • Electrical: movement of charged particles
  • Mechanical: energy that is directly involved in moving matter
  • Radiant: travels in waves

In any conversion of energy, we lose some energy to heat.

In the body, chemical energy of foods is trapped in the bonds of a high-energy chemical called ATP (adenosine triphosphate).
