Why is Christian Ethics important?

We make ethical decisions every day.

We confront ethical issues in society.

We live in a "postmodern" world where values are inverted: "...evil is called good and good is called evil" (Isaiah 5:20)

"The theory of relativity is considered an absolute norm and biblical absolutes are considered relative."

To clarify the issues

We must deal with issues on the basis of our character and values...One task of Christian ethics is formation of character.

  • What factors lead a person to a decision?
  • How do we calculate factors such as where a person is born and grows up?
  • Who are a person's models or heroes?
  • What exercises authority in one's life?
  • How has one managed experiences of pain?
  • How has the person experienced and managed power or influence?
  • How has the person experienced blessing in life?

Individuals express their perspective on the Christian life in different ways

  • "Thou shalt not..." A basic level of ethical reflection: there are things that Christians must not do
  • "Thou shalt..." More positive, but still places primary responsibility for decisions outside of the person
  • "I must..." Sees personal responsibility ind decisions; but still has a strong orientation of duty...There's no joy in decisions
  • "I'll do it/I won't do it" Recognizes positive and negative imperatives in the Christian life - a sense of duty, but also joy and hope in the decision
  • "I am..." The image that Paul gives: "the perfect man" - a mature person in Christ
