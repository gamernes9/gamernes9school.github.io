Ethics and Philosophy (Chapter 2)

Main idea & terms

Ethical theories/systems have been divided into 3 general areas through history

Major terms:

  • Metaethics/Epistemology of Ethics
  • Applied Ethics
  • Classical Greek Philosophers (Plato, Aristotle)
  • Medieval Philosophers (Augustine, Aquinas)
  • Modern Philosophers (Hume, Kant)

Points

  • There are points of agreement and disagreement between the Christian worldview and secular philosophies concerning ethics.

Ethics in modern Western society

Ethics describes normative behavior - what one should do in various situations.

"Ethics" and "morality" often are used interchangeably, however there are distinctions:

  • Ethics describes what is normative or absolute; it's a set of stand
  • Morality describes shifting behavioral patterns of society.
  • Ethics is more concerned with what people ought to do; morality, what people already do.

Three general categories of ethics

Metaethics/Epistemology of Ethics

  • Studies source and meaning of ethical principles
  • Examples of meta-ethical questions:
    • What is the meaning of words like right, good, just?
    • How do we know what is right and wrong?

Normative Ethics

  • More practical study of ethics, with goal of arriving at moral principles for human conduct
  • Examples of questions:
    • What actions are good and bad?
    • What should we do?

Applied Ethics

  • Applies normative ethics to specific, often controversial, issues
