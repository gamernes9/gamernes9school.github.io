8-22-17 - Chapter 1

What is Christian Ethics?

God gives us specific instructions about how he wants us to conduct our lives

  • including commands, laws, moral standards, ideals, prohibitions, and principles
  • concerning moral accountability & judgment, rewards & punishments
  • in order to please God by applying the holiness of God to human life (Lev. 11:44-45; 19:2; 20:7; 1 Peter 1:14-16)

Brief Overview of Biblical Ethics

The first example of ethical instruction in the Bible: God gave Adam & Eve commands

  • Positively. "Be fruitful and multiply, and fill the earth, and subdue it..." (Gen. 1:28).
  • Negatively. "from the tree of the knowledge of good and evil you shall not eat" (Gen. 1:27)

Later God gave Israel the foundation of his law in the Ten Commandments (Ex. 20) then added more detailed laws in Exodus, Leviticus, Numbers & Deuteronomy - proscribing and prohibiting certain actions, as well as teaching right attitudes: "You shall love the Lord your God with all your heart and all your soul and with all your might. These words, which I am commanding you today, shall be on your heart" (Deut. 6:5-6)

The Bible teaches about moral living through narrative (examples of what pleases and displeases God), wisdom literature (examples of good judgment), and prophecy (God's call to repent from sin and indications of consequences of repenting of sin and continuing in sin).

Jesus came & lived a life of perfect obedience to his Father.

  • "I do nothing on My own initiative, but I speak these things as the Father taught Me. And HE who sent ME is with ME; He has not left Me alone, for I always do the things that are pleasing to Him" (John 8:28-29).
  • "Jesus the Son of God...has been tempted in all things as we are, yet without sin" (Heb. 4:14-15).

Faith & Obedience

Justification, or pardon & acceptance with God, comes to people through faith in Christ alone, who gave himself on the cross to make possible our pardon by God's grace alone: "by grace you have been saved through faith; and that not of yourselves, it is the gift of God; not as a result of works, lest any man should boast"

God's Holiness as His Moral Standard

God is constantly and consistently true to his own nature.

His moral standard is always consistent with his nature and character (in general: 1 Peter 1:15; 1 John 4:19; Luke 6:36; Eph. 5:1)...More specific commandments and laws also follow from and reflect his character.

Anything contrary to God's character is sin, or rebellion against God.

God actively opposes, constantly & consistently, all sin or evil.
