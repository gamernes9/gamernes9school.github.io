9-12-17 Chapter 3

God reveals himself through our conscience (Rom. 2:12-16) - as we are created in his image (Gen. 1:26-27) - "Natural Revelation"

God reveals himself through his spoken and written word (Psalm 19:7-11; Psalm 119; 2 Tim. 3:16; 2 Peter 1:20-21) - "Special Revelation"

God has revelaed himself ultimately through his Son, Jesus Christ, the incarnate and Living Word (Heb. 1:1-3) - "Special Revelation"

Why is this foundation - God's self-revelation - necessary for Christian ethics?

God's revealed nature is the ultimate, absolute, and sole standard of right and wrong (Isaiah 6:1-9; Rom. 3:23)

So, as we consider ethical issues in our contemporary culture, we take into account historical - philosophical perspectives, but the Bible is always our primary source and guide

  • "See to it that no one takes you captive through philosophy and empty deception, according to the tradition of men, according to the elementary principles of the world, rather than according to Christ" (i.e., anything against the gospel) (Col. 2:8)

What's the most basic principle for Christian ethics?

Love God, and love your neighbor as yourself

What do you think a "principle" is when we're talking about scripture?

A biblical truth which can be applied universally (to all people, anywhere, at any time)

Most of the Old Testament consists of the story of God's dealings with Israel, based on his covenant with them (Ex. 24)

OT ethics are expressed especially (but not exclusively) in the form of legal codes by which God have direction for social conduct on many levels.

OT law is usually divided into 3 major categories: ceremonial, civil, and moral
