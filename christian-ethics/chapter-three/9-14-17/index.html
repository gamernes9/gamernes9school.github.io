Methods of doing NT ethics

The entire Bible as a book of laws or commandments for controlling conduct

  • Indeed, Jesus' ethical teaching was given with the force of commandments and not merely suggestions
  • This method's limitation is that it doesn't produce morally committed persons, but individuals who struggle to control the worst of immoral conduct
  • Although this method can help in the area of personal morality, it doesn't give sufficient direction in the area of social morality

The Bible merely as a collection of moral principles

  • Permits flexibility in the relevant application of Scripture for today
  • However, it makes Jesus look like a mere philosopher who spoke about various moral issues

Making decisions as an "encounter" with God through the Holy Spirit

  • It's good to affirm the Holy Spirit's role in guiding the believer, but it's dangerous to make decisions in only a subjective manner

Considering circumstances to determine a "loving response"

  • Indeed, the law of love should be applied in every situation
  • However, a limitation is that "love" can become mere sentiment, variously defined by individuals, and used to tolerate whatever kind of conduct

Each of these methods contains an element of truth that should be utilized in an adequate approach

  • The 1st method emphasizes that God has provided an authoritative source for guidance
  • The 2nd affirms that the Bible provides principles instead of narrowly applied rules
  • The 3rd affirms that the Holy Spirit's role is indispensable for living an ethical life
  • The 4th emphasizes the importance of the motive of love in a life that pleases God
