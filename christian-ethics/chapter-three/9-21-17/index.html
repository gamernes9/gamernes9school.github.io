9-21-17 Chapter 3

Managing difficult issues

Christians may disagree over some issues (Rom. 14:1-2; 1 Cor. 8:1-13)

The primary goal for Christians in everything: glorify God (1. Cor. 10:31)...within those parameters, there is liberty on some issues

A Christian should avoid what could offend one's own conscience...some things, though morally neutral, could be obstacles in one's personal relationship with God or others (1. Cor. 6:12)

The emphasis of all of James's letter: conduct that demonstrates authentic faith

He does not teach "faith in works", rather "a faith that works"

NT ethics: Conclusion

No NT writer gives a systematic summary of ethics...rather they provide principles

Example:

  • Writing to Philemon, Paul doesn't attack the to the institution of slavery, rather he contributes to revolutionizing the thinking of Christians.
  • In Colossians 4:1, he warned owners of their responsibility to their slaves, and in 3:22, spoke to slaves as responsible toward God
  • In Philemon, he presented Onesimus as a Christian brother, instead of a slave...when an owner can think of a slave as a brother, the legal designation of "slave" will no longer have significance
  • Paul first connected Philemon and Onesimus in Christian love in such a way that emancipation would become necessary...exposed to the light of the gospel, the institution of slavery would have to shrivel and die.
