Jeremy Bentham (1748-1832)

  • Developed system on idea of pleasure, based on ancient hedonism, which pursued physical pleasure and avoided pain
  • "Nature has placed mankind under the governance of two sovereign masters, pain and pleasure...they govern us in all we do, in all we say, in all we think..."

John Stuart Mill (1806-1873)

  • Developed on Bentham's system, but added a "qualitative element, suggesting that some pleasures were of higher quality than others
  • Proposed that rules could be developed based on what has produced the best consequence for the most people in the past...(but ultimately focus on results demolishes the rules)

Significant weakness of Utilitarianism

  • Remains, in spite of attempts to "quantify", very subjective and based more on emotion than logic and consistent principles
  • Offers no protection for the minority
  • Predicting the ends or consequences is based on guesswork

Situation Ethics

A form of utilitarianism, first proposed by Joseph Fletcher (1905-1991), who modified the pleasure principle and called it the agape (love) principle...but it was still the same utilitarianism

The law of love requires the greatest love for the greatest number of people in the long run

Tried to find alternatives to extremes of legalism and antinomianism

Misinterpreted and misapplied biblical passages to support his ideas (e.g. Matthew 22:37-40)...missed or ignored Matthew 5:17-19
