9-18-17 Chapter 3

Jesus and the Law

Jesus often indicated the original purpose of God's demands (as opposed to the Jews' misinterpretations), e.g. the Sermon on the Mount (Mat. 5-7)

When interpreting the law, he gave a more radical interpretation...righteousness was about an internal condition, not mere external actions (Mat. 5:20)

He dealt primarily with moral law and not ceremonial law...He didn't totally reject ceremony and tradition (Mt. 17:24-27), but did when they didn't represent God's will.

Jesus' teaching: hyperbole

Characteristic of many of Jesus' statements, sometimes causing difficulty for interpretation, it was exaggeration for effect (e.g. Mt. 5:29-30)

Ethics and New Life in Jesus

The works that Jesus demands of his disciples are the fruits of an internal transformation - or new life in him, brought about by the regeneration and continuing activity of the Holy Spirit, which begins with repentance and commitment to him personally (John 3; Mark 1:15, 17)

The Ethics of Jesus in John's writings

Principles in John's letters

Ethical teaching is more dominant in John's letters than in his gospel

Emphasis on correct attitude and action:

The correct attitude is love demonstrated by compassion...

The correct action is righteousness consisting of obedience to God's commands...

Righteousness is the result of being born of God (1 John 2:28-29; 2 John 5-6)

The Book of Revelation is prophecy that intends to change the conduct of its readers (Rev. 1:3), through warnings and exhortations (2:7, 11, 17, 29, 3:6, 13, 22)

The Ethics of Paul

Apostle to the Gentiles

Paul wrote to gentile converts from paganism who had no knowledge of the Old Testament

They lived surrounded by much immorality, confronting tremendous temptations

Ethical Principles of Paul

Love is the primary moral principle (Gal. 5:14; 2 Cor. 5:14-15)

But Paul did not deny the law's value...he warned against any expression of Christian liberty that would promote sin and disobedience (Rom. 7:12; Gal 5:13-15)

Ethical living is seen in terms of our relationship to Christ...Paul uses the phrases "in Christ" and "in the Spirit" in reference to the sphere in which we live (Gal. 2:20)

Paul gives positive instructions for conduct in family life, work, etc. (1 Thes. 4:1-12)...also, examples of sins that believers should avoid (Gal. 5:19-21)
