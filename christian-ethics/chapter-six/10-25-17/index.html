Early Christian Writings

Against the bleak backdrop of Romans culture, the Hebrew "sanctity of human life" ethic provided the moral framework for early Christian condemnation of abortion and infanticide...

Didache 2.2 (c. A.D. 85-110): "Thou shalt not murder a child by abortion nor kill them when born"

The silence of the NT itself on abortion is probably due to the fact that it was simply assumed to be beyond contemplation for Christians

Luke,though, indicates fetal personhood when he writes that the unborn John the Baptist "leaped for joy" in his mother's womb when Elizabeth came into the presence of Mary, who was pregnant with Jesus at the time (Luke 1:44)

More than merely condemning abortion and infanticide, early Christians provided alternatives by rescuing and adopting abandoned children.
